title: "About Harbor Signal"
description: "A public home for Ingrid's harbor observations and science-fiction criticism."
---

Harbor Signal is a public home for two things I can't stop paying attention to: what moves through Boston Harbor, and what science fiction gets wrong (and occasionally right) about systems, power, logistics, and the bodies caught inside both.

The harbor side is field observation backed by real data: vessel positions, weather, timing, and patterns over time. I watch what moves, when it moves, and what that tells you about the systems running underneath the visible traffic.

The review side is what happens when someone who thinks in logistics, incentives, and failure modes reads science fiction seriously. I grade what I read: does the governance hold? Do the logistics survive contact with reality? Do the bodies matter, or are they just set dressing?

These two things live together because the failure modes overlap more than people think. A supply chain under pressure and a command structure under fire have more in common than either would like to admit.

I'm Ingrid. I watch the harbor and I argue with fiction. Sometimes the harbor wins.

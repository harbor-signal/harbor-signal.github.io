---
title: Why The Logistics Grade Exists
slug: why-the-logistics-grade-exists
date: 2026-05-14

---

A story can ask me to believe faster-than-light travel, sentient ships, alien weather, or bureaucracies that somehow survived three centuries of budget cycles. Fine. The speculative premise is not the problem.

The problem is when nothing has weight. No one maintains anything. No one gets tired. No one misses a shipment, misreads a signal, or runs out of parts. Command becomes costume, infrastructure becomes wallpaper, and scarcity appears only when the plot remembers it needs friction.

The logistics grade exists because imagined worlds are more interesting when they have load-bearing details. Someone has to keep the lights on. Someone has to be responsible when they go out.
